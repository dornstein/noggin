# Research notes

Short, opinionated summaries of the cognitive-science literature
that grounds noggin's design. Each topic has its own file. The
goal is enough depth to act on, not a comprehensive review.

## Topics

| Topic | Why it matters for noggin |
|---|---|
| [Working memory & cognitive load](working-memory.md) | Why externalization is required at all — the bottleneck noggin exists to relieve. |
| [Interruption & task switching](interruption.md) | The cost of context-switching, and what tools can do about it. |
| [Prospective memory](prospective-memory.md) | Remembering to do future things — and why external cues outperform internal ones. |
| [External cognition & distributed cognition](external-cognition.md) | The theoretical basis for "the file is part of your mind." |
| [Goal hierarchies & cognitive architectures](goal-hierarchies.md) | Why a tree of nested goals matches how humans actually plan. |
| [Metacognition & reflective practice](metacognition.md) | Why preserving the log matters — reflection requires a past to look at. |
| [Attention, flow & personal information management](attention-flow-pim.md) | Why the tool itself must not become an interruption; why plain-text local files outlast walled gardens. |
| [AI assistants and cognition](ai-and-cognition.md) | The newest literature: cognitive offloading risks specific to working with LLMs, and what to do about them. |

## Convention

Each file is structured as:

1. **The core claim(s)** in plain language.
2. **Key sources** with full citations.
3. **Implications for noggin** — the bridge from research to
   product behaviour.
4. **Caveats / contested points** where relevant.

Citations are a mix of primary sources (the seminal papers) and
secondary summaries (review articles, textbooks). When a claim is
contested, that's flagged here and a question is logged in
[open-questions.md](../open-questions.md).
